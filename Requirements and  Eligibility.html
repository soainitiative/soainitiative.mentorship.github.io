S.O.A Initiative

Requirements and Eligibility

Mentee Eligibility

  • You need to be in Grade 10, 11, or 12 and beyond.
  • You must show a strong interest in pursuing a healthcare career.
  • Be open to participating in career exploration activities, such as virtual job shadowing and informational interviews with healthcare professionals.

Mentor Eligibility

  • You must be currently enrolled in a university program (certificate, diploma, undergraduate, or graduate) or already working in the healthcare field.
  • The program is open to students from their second year to graduation.
  • You should demonstrate a clear interest in healthcare through things like coursework, volunteering, or part-time work.
  • Be ready to engage in more hands-on mentorship, including opportunities such as shadowing, internships, and career development discussions.

Commitment

  • Mentees must be able to dedicate time to the program for a set period, typically between 3 to 6 months.
  • Regular communication with mentors is expected, including check-ins through virtual or in-person meetings.

Program Participation

  • Mentees should actively participate in career exploration activities, including discussions with healthcare professionals and hands-on experiences.
  • Engagement and reliability are key. Both parties should be committed to making the most out of the mentorship experience.
